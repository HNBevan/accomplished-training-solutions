Professional Fire Safety Training

Accomplished Training Solutions delivers comprehensive fire safety training courses across the whole of the UK. All our fire safety courses meet current UK fire safety legislation requirements including the Regulatory Reform (Fire Safety) Order 2005. We bring expert training directly to your workplace, saving time and reducing costs.

Our experienced trainers provide practical, engaging courses that ensure your team understands fire safety procedures, emergency evacuation protocols, and their responsibilities in preventing and responding to fire emergencies. Certificates are issued on the same day of training completion.

Fire Warden Training

Half Day Valid for 1 Year

Our Fire Warden Training course provides designated fire wardens with the essential knowledge and practical skills to fulfill their fire safety responsibilities. This half-day course covers everything needed to effectively manage fire safety in the workplace.

Course Content

  • Understanding fire warden roles and responsibilities
  • Fire safety legislation and workplace requirements
  • Fire prevention and risk awareness
  • Fire extinguisher types and their uses
  • Emergency evacuation procedures
  • Assembly point management
  • Conducting fire drills
  • Communication during emergencies
  • Identifying fire hazards in the workplace

Who Should Attend?

This course is essential for designated fire wardens, fire marshals, health and safety officers, and any employee with fire safety responsibilities. Every workplace should have trained fire wardens on each floor or in each department.

Fire Marshal Training

1 Day Valid for 1 Year

Our comprehensive Fire Marshal Training course provides in-depth knowledge for those taking senior responsibility for fire safety. This full-day course builds on fire warden training with additional leadership and management responsibilities.

Course Content

  • Advanced fire safety legislation and compliance
  • Fire marshal roles, duties and legal responsibilities
  • Comprehensive fire risk assessment principles
  • Fire prevention strategies and control measures
  • Fire detection and alarm systems
  • Fire extinguisher selection and practical use
  • Emergency evacuation planning and management
  • Managing evacuations for vulnerable persons
  • Coordinating with emergency services
  • Fire safety inspections and reporting
  • Managing and training fire wardens
  • Record keeping and documentation

Who Should Attend?

Ideal for senior fire marshals, facilities managers, health and safety managers, building managers, and those with overall responsibility for fire safety management in larger premises or multi-floor buildings.

Fire Safety Awareness

2-3 Hours Certificate Provided

Our Fire Safety Awareness course provides all employees with essential fire safety knowledge. This condensed training ensures everyone in your workplace understands basic fire safety principles and emergency procedures.

Course Content

  • Basic fire safety principles and the fire triangle
  • Common causes of fires in the workplace
  • Fire prevention and good housekeeping
  • Understanding fire alarms and warnings
  • Emergency evacuation procedures
  • Assembly points and roll calls
  • Fire exit routes and signage
  • Basic fire extinguisher awareness
  • Personal responsibilities for fire safety
  • Reporting fire hazards and concerns

Who Should Attend?

Essential for all employees, new starters requiring induction training, office staff, retail workers, and anyone working in a shared building. This training helps fulfill your legal duty to provide fire safety information to all staff.

Fire Risk Assessment Training

1 Day Valid for 3 Years

Our Fire Risk Assessment Training course provides the knowledge and skills to conduct thorough fire risk assessments in accordance with UK legislation. This comprehensive course is essential for those responsible for fire safety management and compliance.

Course Content

  • Fire safety legislation and legal requirements
  • The five steps of fire risk assessment
  • Identifying fire hazards and ignition sources
  • Identifying people at risk
  • Evaluating and reducing fire risks
  • Recording findings and creating action plans
  • Implementing and monitoring control measures
  • Emergency evacuation planning
  • Fire safety management systems
  • Reviewing and updating risk assessments
  • Documentation and record keeping
  • Practical risk assessment exercises

Who Should Attend?

Essential for responsible persons under the Regulatory Reform Order, facilities managers, health and safety managers, fire safety officers, building managers, and anyone required to conduct or review fire risk assessments.

Frequently Asked Questions

For designated Fire Wardens, specialized training must be renewed annually. General employee Fire Safety awareness training is typically conducted every 12-18 months.

The Fire Warden (or Fire Marshal) is responsible for ensuring the orderly and safe evacuation of their designated area, performing a sweep of the area, and assisting with the head count at the assembly point.

Fire doors are a critical safety feature. They are designed to compartmentalize the building and stop the spread of fire and smoke for a specified period, giving people more time to escape. Never prop them open.
